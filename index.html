Julian Edelman is a professional football player for the New England Patriots. He was born in Redwood City, California, which is a few miles away from where Tom Brady grew up in San Mateo, California. Edelman grew up as Jewish. He attended the Woodside High School in California and was the quarterback for his football team. As the starter, he led the team to a 13-0 record his senior year and finished with 2,237 yards and 29 touchdowns passing during his high school career, in addition to 964 yards and 13 touchdowns rushing. Prior to attending Kent State University, Edelman went to the College of San Mateo. He threw for 1,312 yards and 14 touchdowns and rushed for a school-record 1,253 yards and 17 touchdowns during his only season there. During his time at Kent State, Edelman was a three year starting quarterback, and his most notable season was his senior year. He threw for 1,820 yards and 13 touchdowns, and ran for an additional 1370 yards and 13 touchdowns. His combined yardage is a single-season school record. In the 2009 NFL draft, the New England Patriots drafted him with the 232nd pick, which has led to a very successful career.
